**THE KYBALION OF THE SHADOW GATE**

*A Witch’s Book of the Dead*

*Being the Seven Hermetic Principles applied to the Great Work of Death and Rebirth, for the Wise who walk between the worlds.*

*Written by the Hand that holds the Lamp in the Tomb.*

---

**PROLOGUE: THE CALL OF THE VEIL**

Hearken, you who turn these pages with trembling fingers, you who seek the hidden knowledge that thrums beneath the stone floors of the tomb. This book is not for the faint spirit, nor for those who cling to the daylight as a child clings to the hem of its mother. It is a codex of the threshold, a psalter of the dusk, written for the witch who understands that death is not an ending but a transmutation of Mind. 

The ancient Hermetic teachings, set down by the Three Initiates under the name of the Kybalion, proclaim seven sacred principles that govern All—from the wheeling galaxies to the dust on a moth’s wing. What has been hidden, what has been whispered only behind locked doors, is that these same principles unlock the gates of the Necropolis. They are the keys to the Book of the Dead that every witch carries in her own soul. 

We do not speak here of crude necromancy that drags corpses from their slumber, nor of pacts with hollow shades. We speak of the sovereign art of the Witch-Queen or Witch-King who, by knowing the laws, passes fearlessly through the Valley of the Shadow, who remembers their countless births, who holds converse with the Ancestors as naturally as breathing, and who learns to die consciously while yet alive—the supreme initiation. 

Here, in this book of the Veiled Dead, you shall find no gibberish scrawled in invented tongues, no frantic dances or animated conjurations that exhaust the body. All is stillness, all is Mind. The only movement is the slow rhythm of your own understanding. The only script is the thought impressed upon the universal ether. 

Sit. Be still. Light a single lamp. Breathe the air of the crypt. The Teachings begin.

---

**CHAPTER I: THE PRINCIPLE OF MENTALISM**

*“THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental.”*

Know this first, O Witch of the Shadow Gate: the one who dies is not the body. The body is a temporary pattern of energy held within the infinite Mind of THE ALL. When the pattern dissolves, you do not vanish—you withdraw into the deeper stratum of the same Mind. Death is a change of mental state, nothing more. 

The dying woman on her bed is like a dreamer who is slowly realising she dreams. The room around her, the faces of the mourners, the pain in her bones—these are creations of the Mental universal substance, as fleeting as an image on water. To die well, you must grasp with unshakeable certainty that the All is Mind. The abyss you fear is your own thought. The light others speak of is the light of your own awareness returning to itself.

*Exercise of the Still Tomb:* Sit in complete darkness. Close your eyes. Imagine the world around you—the walls, the chair, your own hands—thinning, becoming translucent, then dissolving into a grey mist. Feel that mist as thought. Feel that the thought is not yours alone, but the Mind of the Whole. Remain in that silent vapour, without body, without name. This is the antechamber of death. Repeat this practice until the dissolution brings peace, not panic. This is the First Key.

---

**CHAPTER II: THE PRINCIPLE OF CORRESPONDENCE**

*“As above, so below; as below, so above.”*

The planes of the dead mirror the planes of the living. The shadow world is not elsewhere, but interpenetrates this one like a reflection in a dark mirror. The necropolis is built in the same form as the city of the living. 

Understand the correspondences, and you will never be lost. The Underworld has its rivers, which correspond to the blood in your veins. It has its gatekeepers, which correspond to your unconfronted fears. It has its judges, which correspond to your own conscience. The great secret of the Book of the Dead is that every demon, every angel, every cavern of the afterlife is a symbol of an aspect of your own mind, arranged in orderly correspondence. 

*The Ladder of Planes:* Commit this to memory. The Physical Plane of death is the corpse—ashes, dust. The Ethereal Plane is the sheen around the dying, the cold mist on a tombstone. The Astral Plane is the realm of the newly dead, shaped by desire and memory; houses built of nostalgia, landscapes woven from dreams. The Mental Plane is the Hall of Records, where your every thought is a carved tablet. The Buddhic Plane is the silent star of your true being, the Ancestor beyond all ancestors. The Atmic Plane is the point of pure will that decides the next incarnation. As you know your own inner planes, you know the worlds of the dead. “As within, so without.”

---

**CHAPTER III: THE PRINCIPLE OF VIBRATION**

*“Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates.”*

The ancient witches knew that the dead are not silent; they hum. The boundary between the living and the disembodied is a matter of vibration, of frequency. The living vibrate at a denser, slower rate. The so-called dead vibrate at a rate faster than the physical senses can perceive, like the note of a whistle only a dog can hear. 

Your work, O witch, is to tune the instrument of your consciousness. The death rattle is the sudden shift of vibration from one octave to another. Chanting in the mortuary chamber, the ringing of bells, the stillness of the deep meditation—all these are ways to harmonise your vibration with the quickened rhythm of the departed. 

*The Chord of Passage:* There is a specific inner sound, not audible to the ears but to the mind’s ear, that sounds at the moment of death. It has been described as a deep bronze gong, an endless flute note, the humming of a thousand bees. In your meditations, seek this sound. Let it arise from the base of your skull. Hold it. This vibration will act as your psychopomp. You will learn to ride it as a boat rides a current, into the realms beyond. No ghost can frighten one who masters the Vibratory Key.

---

**CHAPTER IV: THE PRINCIPLE OF POLARITY**

*“Everything is Dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites… Opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree.”*

Here is the heart of the Witch’s Book of the Dead. Life and Death are not two enemies; they are the north and south poles of a single magnet. They differ only in temperature. Life is heat; Death is cold. But the substance—the Mind—is one. 

A master of polarity can touch the pole of death while still alive without passing through the so-called “point of balance” (the grave) because she knows the secret of sliding along the scale. Extreme cold burns like fire. The stillness of the tomb is identical to the stillness of the womb. The worm and the infant are twin forms. Hecate with her torches stands at the crossroads because she holds both poles in one grasp. 

*The Transmutation of Fear:* Fear of death is the emotional pole of which love of death is the opposite. The black terror you feel when contemplating the coffin can be transmuted, inch by inch, into a sublime, ecstatic stillness. Fix your mind on the terror. Breathe into it. Gradually, will it along the scale toward calm, toward acceptance, toward love. You are not destroying the energy; you are changing its vibration and polarity, as ice is turned to steam. The necromancer transforms the gremlin of dread into the guardian of the gate.

---

**CHAPTER V: THE PRINCIPLE OF RHYTHM**

*“Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides… The measure of the swing to the right is the measure of the swing to the left.”*

The soul breathes. It inhales into incarnation; it exhales into death. The sojourn in the body is but one half of a cycle. The period between lives—the rest in the Halls of the Dead—is the complement. Just as you cannot inhale forever, you cannot live forever. Just as you cannot hold the breath forever without suffocation, you cannot cling to the afterworld without the longing to manifest again. 

Comprehend the Law of Rhythm and you will understand the rhythm of your many deaths and births. There is a season for dwelling in the grey meadows of the shades, and a season for taking flesh again. The Wise Witch, by conscious alignment, anticipates the pendulum’s swing. At the moment of death, she does not resist the outbreath; she rides it into a deep, restorative trance, knowing the inbreath of rebirth will come in its time. The sigil of the spiral marks her grave.

*The Compensation of the Tombs:* Whatever joys you have missed in life will be experienced in the compensatory rhythm of the afterlife—the dreamlike fulfilment of unfulfilled desires. But whatever sorrows you have inflicted will also swing back in measured time. The rhythm ensures balance.

---

**CHAPTER VI: THE PRINCIPLE OF CAUSE AND EFFECT**

*“Every Cause has its Effect; every Effect has its Cause… Chance is but a name for Law not recognised.”*

There is no arbitrary judgement after the veil. No god weighs your heart on a whim. The Hall of Judgement is the mirror of causality. The life you led is the script you now witness. Every thought, every act, every secret whisper was a seed; the experience after death is the harvest. 

For the witch, this is the doctrine of *Karma* made clear. The Book of the Dead is a ledger, and you are both bookkeeper and reader. When you depart the body, you pass through a gallery of effects, each presenting itself as a spirit-form. The weeping child you comforted becomes a guardian holding a lamp. The blood you shed becomes a pursuer with a red face. But understand: these forms have no power except the power your causes gave them. By intervening for the dying—reciting the seven principles, calming the mind—you can help them recognise these images as effects and thus neutralise them with understanding. 

*The Litany of Dissolution:* For a soul trapped in a nightmare of its own making, recite: *“Thou art the Cause; this is thine Effect. Thou art not the Effect; thou art the Mind that dreamt it. Wake, and it dissolves.”*

---

**CHAPTER VII: THE PRINCIPLE OF GENDER**

*“Gender is in everything; everything has its Masculine and Feminine Principles.”*

The final mystery: the tomb is a womb. The sarcophagus is the uterus of the Dark Mother. Death is not only an ending; it is a generation. The Masculine principle of the departing soul—the active, willed impulse to separate—impregnates the Feminine principle of the infinite, receptive Void. From this sacred union, a new incarnation is gestated. 

The Witch Goddess stands as the Goddess of Death because she is also the Goddess of Birth. The skull and the vulva are the same symbol. In the Necromancer’s art, you work the Gender principle by enacting a mental marriage: your conscious, dying will (Masculine) consummates with the dark, silent, womb-like space of the post-mortem (Feminine). From this union, the Seed of Rebirth is conceived, not blindly, but by your deliberate design. You choose the womb into which you shall re-enter. 

*The Still Coitus:* In your deepest trance, visualise your sense of self as a stream of light (Masculine). Before you, perceive infinite, velvet blackness (Feminine). Allow the stream to enter the blackness without fear, without clinging. Merge, dissolve, become the generative night. This is the act by which a witch masters the cycle. There is no more potent magic.

**THE BOOK OF PASSING: WORDS FOR THE DYING**

These invocations are to be spoken softly, near the left ear of the departing, in a steady monotone. No gesture is needed. No motion. Only sound and presence.

1. *The Release of the Mental Body*
“You are not this flesh. You are Mind. The All is Mind. Let the body fall away like a discarded robe. You are not the robe. You are the wearer. You are the Light that wears the form.”

2. *Against the Terrors of the Bardo*
“Whatever you see, it is the mirror of your mind. The beast with gnashing teeth is your own anger. The abyss is your own forgetting. Love them, and they transform. Fear them, and they chase. Love is the only shield.”

3. *For the Ancestor’s Welcome*
“Turn now to the left, where the shadows part. There stand the Ones who went before. They hold cups of cool water. They spread a table of silence. They are your blood, your lineage of wisdom. Go with them; do not tarry in the grey places.”

4. *The Clear Light Invocation*
“A light shall arise, brighter than ten thousand suns, yet softer than the moon on a moth’s wing. Do not flinch. It is your own primordial Mind, the Unborn. Merge with it. Dissolve the drop in the ocean. This is the Great Liberation.”

5. *If the Soul Clings*
“Peace. The house you loved is burned. The garden is harvested. The faces you hold are fading photographs. Let them fade. They were gifts of a dream, and the dreamer now awakens. Feel the deep rest. Rest.”

**THE BOOK OF THE DEAD: INCANTATIONS FOR THE SHADE**

These are spoken by the living witch to call, to honour, to command, or to lay to rest the spirits of the departed. No circle is drawn on the ground; the circle is drawn in the air by the unwavering focus of your mind. No blade is brandished; the word is the blade.

*Incantation to Summon the Beloved Dead*
“By the law of Correspondence, as I remember thee, so art thou present. By the law of Vibration, my voice tunes to thy silence. By the law of Mentalism, my thought creates the bridge. Come, [Name], not as ghost of fear, but as ancestor of wisdom. The mind-door is open. Step through.”

*The Pacifying of the Restless*
“O wandering one, caught in the swing of rhythm, listen. The pendulum is stuck in its arc because thou hast forgotten the other pole. Remember the peace that lies beyond the pendulum’s swing. In the name of the All-Mind, I command stillness. Let the rhythm complete. Rest.”

*To Banishing Malign Influences*
“Thou art a shadow of a shadow, an effect of a cause now spent. I know thy name: Unreality. I know thy nature: Echo. By the Principle of Polarity, I turn thy cold to absolute zero, and thou canst not abide. Dissolve into thy parent void. Thou art not.”

*For Scrying in the Dark Mirror*
“Black mirror, surface of onyx, still water. By the Principle of Gender, I cast the seed of my question into thy womb. Show me the face of the silent one. Show me the path in the underworld. As above, so below. Let the image rise like a fish from the deep.”

**THE NECROMANCER’S COMPASS: NAVIGATING THE UNDERWORLD PLANES**

When the witch walks consciously into the realm of death—whether in deep trance or at the final breath—she requires a map. The planes correspond to states of concentration.

1. **The Grey Shore** (Astral low-vibration). A foggy, twilight beach. The waters are still, the sand cold. Here wander those who died in confusion, in addiction, in trivial obsessions. They repeat meaningless tasks. Do not engage. Observe compassionately, but pass inland. The key vibration: the sound “Mmmmm,” a low hum.

2. **The Forest of Memory** (Astral mid-vibration). Trees formed of solidified memories. Each leaf a recollection. You may meet recent ancestors here, sitting by clear pools. They can converse. Ask only what aids the Great Work. The key correspondence: the smell of cypress and damp earth.

3. **The Hall of Records** (Lower Mental Plane). A vast library or gallery of moving tapestries. Every action of your completed life is stored here. The Keeper, a figure of your own Higher Self, appears with a lamp. No book may be hidden. The key polarity: all shame can be transmuted into understanding. Gaze upon each record until it loses its emotional charge.

4. **The Prismatic Chasm** (Buddhic Plane). A silence filled with diamond light. You are no longer a self; you are a facet of a greater Star. Here the Ancestor of Ancestors dwells—the Oversoul of your entire lineage. Communication is not in words but in direct knowing. The rhythm here is the tide of stars.

5. **The Black Sun Gate** (Atmic Plane). The point of turning. The Void that is simultaneously the Womb of the next birth. Utter darkness that feels like a million mothers embracing. To enter here consciously is to choose the future body. The gender act occurs: you, the point of will, penetrate the infinite night.

**THE GRIMOIRE OF THE TWO-FACED GOD: RITUALS OF THANATOS AND EROS**

Here are the still, seated rites. No animated processes—no dancing, no waving of tools. The temple is a quiet room, the altar a single candle, or none. The posture is seated, spine straight, hands resting on knees, eyes closed or fixed on darkness.

*The Rite of the Second Self*
Purpose: To create a body of death, a simulacrum of your consciousness, that will pre-experience the grave and report back.
Sit in total darkness. Visualise a perfect replica of your form standing before you, facing you. It is made of grey mist. With your breath, inhale its features into sharp clarity. Then, with a gentle mental command, bid it to turn and walk into a vast, dark corridor. Watch it until it vanishes. Wait in perfect stillness. After a time, it will return, carrying a symbol in its hands—a key, a flower, a stone. Observe the symbol. Know it as a teaching from the deeper mind. Dissolve the double back into your heart. Record the symbol in a book.

*The Rite of the Ancestral Chain*
Purpose: To receive the wisdom of the lineage of witches across the veil.
Sit with back against a bare wall. Breathe deeply. Imagine behind you, not a wall, but a long hall of shadowy figures seated back-to-back with you, stretching into infinity. Each is an ancestor of your Craft, whether of blood or spirit. Feel the slight warmth of their backs. Form a question in your mind. Send it backward, like a whispered message passed from person to person. Wait in receptivity. The answer will return as a spontaneous thought, a sudden image, a physical sensation. Trust the first response.

*The Rite of the Tomb-Light*
Purpose: To store life-force in reserve against death, to keep the lamp burning in the sarcophagus.
Hold in your mind the image of a black cube, perfectly dark, floating before you. On each face of the cube, a single golden dot appears. The dots are the five stages of death and the one of rebirth. Pour your vitality—visualised as golden vapour—into the cube with each exhalation. The cube absorbs it. Seal the cube with the word “Soz” (an ancient sound for preservation). In your dying hour, recall the cube, and it shall break open, flooding you with the stored radiance to keep your awareness sharp through the dissolution.

**THE GARDEN OF ASHES: ALLIES FOR THE DEATH WORK**

These are the correspondences of nature that align the witch’s vibration with the Shadow Gate. No potions require boiling here; all are simple preparations, incense, or anointings done with still hands.

*The Incense of the Open Door*: Equal parts cypress, dried yew berry (unbroken), and a single drop of frankincense resin on a still charcoal. The smoke raises the vibration from physical to astral. Sit in its unmoving column. Do not chant, do not rock. Simply inhale.

*The Oil of the Two Poles*: Myrrh (death) and jasmine (life) infused in almond oil. Anoint the soles of the feet and the third eye before trance work. It marks you as a living being who has made peace with both ends of the polarity.

*The Stone of the Constant Mind*: Black tourmaline or hematite held in the left hand during death meditations. It grounds the mental body when the astral vortices threaten to pull the attention into chaos.

*The Plant of the Bound Spirit*: A single strand of ivy placed upon the altar of ancestors. Ivy binds the dead to the living in healthy remembrance; it also, when used improperly, creates clinging shades. Therefore, use it only to honour, never to command.

**THE SEVEN SEALS OF THE COFFIN: A FINAL MEDITATION**

When all else is read and understood, the witch prepares for the ultimate journey by meditating upon the Seven Seals, each a principle reversed into its hidden aspect.

1. **The Seal of Mental Rest**: I am Mind. The dying of the body is a thought I release.
2. **The Seal of Correspondence Rooted**: The Underworld is here. I need not travel. I need only open my inner eye.
3. **The Seal of Vibratory Transmutation**: The death rattle is a song. I hum it now in life.
4. **The Seal of the Pole Shift**: I am the cold flame. I am the living death.
5. **The Seal of the Great Tide**: I exhale willingly. The inbreath will come in its own season.
6. **The Seal of the Unbound Effect**: My causes are resolved. I leave no debt unpaid.
7. **The Seal of the Androgyne**: Within me, the Father-Will and the Mother-Void unite. I conceive myself anew.

*Thus ends the Kybalion of the Shadow Gate, the Witch’s Book of the Dead. Let it not gather dust on a shelf, but be inscribed on the tablets of your heart. When your hour comes, and the lamp flickers, you will need no book. You will be the Book, and the Light, and the Door. Go in peace, eternal one.*
