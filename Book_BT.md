# Book VT — The Tarot  
*An Exhaustive Exposition of the 78 Cards of the Tarot, Describing Their Symbols, Attributions, and Divinatory Meanings According to the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn*

---

## Preliminary Remarks

The Tarot is a pictorial representation of the Forces of Nature as conceived by the Ancients according to a conventional symbolism, embodying the Threefold Qabalistic Scheme: the Paths of the Tree of Life, the Letters of the Hebrew Alphabet, and the Astrological Correspondences. The pack consists of **78 cards**:

- **22 Trumps** (Major Arcana) — The great hieroglyphic keys, corresponding to the 22 Paths between the Sephiroth on the Tree of Life, the 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet, and the 12 signs, 7 planets, and 3 elements.
- **16 Court Cards** — The four aspects of each element: Knight, Queen, Prince, Princess.
- **40 Small Cards** — The numbered cards 1 (Ace) through 10 in each of the four suits, corresponding to the 36 Decanates of the Zodiac and the four primordial Aces.

The **four suits** are:
- **Wands** (or Sceptres) — **Fire** (Atziluth, the Archetypal World)
- **Cups** (or Chalices) — **Water** (Briah, the Creative World)
- **Swords** — **Air** (Yetzirah, the Formative World)
- **Pentacles** (or Discs) — **Earth** (Assiah, the Material World)

Each card’s design is a deliberate arrangement of symbols whose keys are: the Hebrew letter, the Path on the Tree of Life (or the Decanate assignment), the astrological ruler, the element, the Color Scale of the Four Worlds, and the mythological or alchemical allegory. In the expanded descriptions that follow, every detail—the posture, the colors, the animals, the implements—is decoded according to the teachings of the Second Order.

### 0 — The Fool  
**Letter:** Aleph (א). **Element:** Air. **Path:** 11th Path, joining Kether (Crown) and Chokmah (Wisdom).  
**Spiritual Title:** The Primum Mobile, the Breath of Life.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A youth clad in motley garments of yellow, green, and red walks with his head uplifted toward the sky. A white feather curling from his hair symbolizes the Airy element and the lightness of divine folly. Over his left shoulder rests a staff, from which hangs a wallet containing the unmanifested potential of the four worlds; the wallet is often embroidered with an eagle’s head. In his right hand, held carelessly downward, is a white rose, the symbol of the purified desire nature. A small white dog leaps at his heels, barking at his movement. The youth appears about to step from the edge of a cliff into a void; behind him a golden sun blazes in a clear sky. The ground is green and fertile at his feet; a chasm drops away before him. In the background, sharp mountain peaks rise, their forms echoing the teeth of the abyss.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The youth:** The Divine Youth, the Androgyne before separation, the soul in a state of innocence and potential. The motley colors reflect the unintegrated elements prior to the formation of the personality.
- **The white feather:** A symbol of Maat, the Egyptian goddess of truth and air, indicating that the Fool moves in accordance with the higher will without calculation.
- **The staff and wallet:** The staff is the Will, the wallet the Bags of the Four Winds from classical myth; within it are the seeds of the four suits, the entire Tarot in potential. The eagle on the wallet refers to the Supernal Triad (the highest Sephiroth) and the element Air.
- **The white rose:** Perfect innocence, the transmuted sexual force, the unfolding of the spiritual heart.
- **The dog:** The guardian of the threshold, the instinctual mind or the karmic companion that attempts to warn the Fool from the leap. In some esoteric interpretations, the dog is an aspect of the Egyptian Anubis.
- **The precipice:** The Abyss between the Supernal and the lower worlds, the leap of faith required to cross from Kether to Chokmah. The Fool is the only card that can traverse the Abyss unscathed.
- **The sun:** Kether, the source of all Light, shining impartially on the soul’s journey.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Absolute trust in the divine, new beginnings, the commencement of a spiritual journey, creative spontaneity, the primal will unbound by logic. Folly that is divine madness.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Recklessness, foolishness for its own sake, mental instability, negligence, thoughtlessness, dissipation of energies.

### I — The Magician  
**Letter:** Beth (ב). **Planet:** Mercury. **Path:** 12th Path, joining Kether and Binah.  
**Spiritual Title:** The Magus of Power, the Manipulator of the Astral Light.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A robed figure stands before a cubic altar, his right hand raising a wand toward the heavens, his left hand pointing to the earth, so that his body forms the glyph of the alchemical “As above, so below.” His robe is white, over which is a crimson mantle, the color of Mercury in the Queen Scale. Around his waist a golden girdle fashioned as an Ouroboros serpent, tail in mouth. Upon his brow is a golden headband, and above his head floats the lemniscate (∞). On the altar before him lie the four elemental weapons: a Wand, a Cup, a Sword, and a Pentacle. Behind him, a trellis of climbing roses, and above, a canopy of the same. The foreground is bright yellow, the Air of the yetziratic world.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **Posture:** The right arm up, left arm down: the descent of the divine fire (the lightning flash) and the ascent of the serpent power. He is the channel between Macrocosm and Microcosm.
- **The wand:** The creative will, double‑terminated, pointing to Kether and to the earth.
- **The altar:** A cube of matter, whose faces are the six directions of space, crowned with the instruments of the four elements, showing that the Magician has mastery over all planes.
- **The lemniscate:** The eternal rhythm of the universe, the cycling of prana, and the nature of Mercury as the swift messenger.
- **The serpent girdle:** Self‑devouring, eternity, the eternal renewal of the mercurial force.
- **The roses and trellis:** The flowering of desire under will (roses = Venus, desire; trellis = the lattice of the mind). Mercury overcomes desire by understanding.
- **The four weapons:** Wand (Fire/Will), Cup (Water/Imagination), Sword (Air/Intellect), Pentacle (Earth/Manifestation) — all are balanced by the central consciousness.
- **Colors:** White (purity), crimson (mercurial activity), gold (solar consciousness), yellow (Air).

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Skill, wisdom, creative intelligence, dexterity, the power to channel spirit into matter. Communication, commerce, scientific cunning.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Trickery, deceit, mental pathology, misuse of talents, the black magician who manipulates for selfish ends.

### II — The High Priestess  
**Letter:** Gimel (ג). **Planet:** Moon. **Path:** 13th Path, joining Kether and Tiphereth.  
**Spiritual Title:** The Priestess of the Silver Star, the Guardian of the Unconscious.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A seated woman, robed in flowing blue and white, her face serene and turned partly away. Upon her head is the lunar crescent crown, which is also the horns of Isis. In her hands she holds a scroll inscribed with the letters TORA, referring to the Law, the Word. She sits between two mighty pillars: the pillar on the right (her left) is of white stone marked with the letter J (Jachin), the pillar on the left (her right) is black marked B (Boaz). Behind her a veil hangs, embroidered with palm leaves and pomegranates in gold and purple. The veil stretches across the temple, hiding the innermost sanctuary. Her throne is set upon a pavement of black and white squares. Beneath her feet, a crescent moon.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The scroll TORA:** The Law of the universe, the divine wisdom that is half‑revealed, half‑concealed. The missing letter “H” in the scroll suggests that the final revelation comes only through union with the Higher Self.
- **The two pillars:** The eternal opposites—Mercy and Severity, Light and Darkness, the Pillar of Cloud and the Pillar of Fire. The Priestess sits in the middle way, the Path of Gimel, the camel, bridging the abyss between Kether and Tiphereth.
- **The veil:** The curtain of Paroketh, which separates the lower Sephiroth from Tiphereth. The palm (triumph) and pomegranate (fertility) suggest that beyond the veil lie the keys to regeneration.
- **The lunar crown:** The Moon, ruler of the tides of the subconscious, of cycles, and of the feminine principle. The horns connect to Isis and Hathor.
- **The black and white pavement:** The duality of manifestation, the checkered floor of the Masonic lodge, the equilibrium of the forces.
- **The crescent underfoot:** Dominion over the sublunary world, the mastery of the astral body.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Wisdom, intuition, spiritual insight, the unveiling of hidden truths, the perfected feminine principle, memory, the reflective mind.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Superficial knowledge, concealment, silence used for deception, psychic disturbance, a false priestess.

### III — The Empress  
**Letter:** Daleth (ד). **Planet:** Venus. **Path:** 14th Path, joining Chokmah and Binah.  
**Spiritual Title:** The Daughter of the Mighty Ones, the Gate of Heaven.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A majestic woman is seated upon a throne, her posture full of grace and authority. She wears a crown of twelve stars, representing the twelve zodiacal signs revolving around the celestial queen. In her right hand she holds a sceptre topped with an orb, the symbol of Venus; her left hand rests upon a shield bearing the glyph of Venus (♀), formed of gold. Her gown is of a rich emerald green, embroidered with golden bees and pomegranates. Before her grows a field of ripe wheat, and behind her a grove of cypress trees rises against a pure blue sky. A stream of living water flows from beneath her throne, meandering through the landscape. The whole scene breathes fecundity, beauty, and the abundance of nature.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The 12-starred crown:** The Queen of Heaven (Mater Coeli) adorned with the twelve constellations, showing that she is the matrix of all forms.
- **The sceptre and shield:** The sceptre is the creative power; the shield bearing the mirror of Venus indicates that the material world is a reflection of the divine. Venus is love and attraction, the binding force of the universe.
- **The emerald robe:** The color of Venus in the Queen Scale, green of nature’s growth. The bees (chastity, industry, community) and pomegranates (fecundity) signify the wisdom of nature’s reproductive order.
- **The wheat field:** Demeter/Ceres, the grain mother, the cycle of death and rebirth in vegetation.
- **The cypress trees:** Sacred to Venus, symbolizing immortality and the eternal nature of the soul; also the axis mundi.
- **The flowing water:** The stream of the Watery element in Yetzirah, the life‑giving fluid of creation, the river that went out from Eden.
- **Path Daleth (door):** The Empress is the Gate of Heaven, the passage from Chokmah (Father) to Binah (Mother) through which the creative Word becomes form.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Fertility, motherhood, beauty, luxury, artistic creation, the power of love, growth, material comfort.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Indolence, sloth, lust, prodigality, smothering possessiveness, neglect of higher things for sensual pleasure.

### IV — The Emperor  
**Letter:** Heh (window) (ה). **Sign:** Aries. **Path:** 15th Path, joining Chokmah and Tiphereth.  
**Spiritual Title:** Son of the Morning, Chief among the Mighty.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A crowned monarch sits upon a massive stone throne, the arms of which are carved as two rams’ heads. He holds in his right hand a sceptre with a ram’s head finial, and in his left the orb of the world, topped with a small cross. His robe is of scarlet, and over his shoulders falls a mantle of purple. Beneath his feet is a platform of red‑orange stone. Behind him rise stark, barren mountains against a sky of deep violet. The Emperor’s countenance is strong and commanding; his red beard and helmet‑like crown convey martial authority. The throne itself is set upon a dais; the emblem of the ram (Aries) is visible on the corner of the throne.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **Rams’ heads and sceptre:** Aries, the cardinal fire sign ruled by Mars, the explosive initiating energy. The ram symbolizes unyielding will, leadership, and conquest.
- **The scarlet robe and purple mantle:** Scarlet is the color of Mars, the planetary lord of Aries, while purple is the sovereign color of Jupiter, the King. Together they show that this Emperor rules by both force and law.
- **The orb and cross:** The world (Malkuth) crowned by the cross of spirit; the Emperor exercises dominion over the material plane through divine right.
- **The barren mountains:** The mineral kingdom, the bones of the earth, the primordial dry land; Aries is the first sign, the spark of life in an uncultivated world.
- **The violet sky:** The color of the King Scale for the Path of Heh, linking the Emperor to the Supernal Father Chokmah.
- **Posture:** He sits, perfectly upright, embodying stability and control, the archetype of paternity, civilization’s foundation.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Authority, dominion, rulership, executive power, courage, protective fatherhood, structure, order.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Tyranny, absolutism, obstinacy, rigidity, weakness disguised as strength, uncontrolled anger.

### V — The Hierophant  
**Letter:** Vau (ו). **Sign:** Taurus. **Path:** 16th Path, joining Chokmah and Chesed.  
**Spiritual Title:** Magus of the Eternal Gods, the Revealer of the Sacred Mysteries.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A figure robed in the vestments of a High Priest, wearing a triple crown (triregnum) and seated between the two pillars of Mercy and Severity, exactly like the High Priestess but with the active, exoteric aspect. His right hand is raised in the gesture of benediction, two fingers extended, two folded, thumb crossed over palm—the esoteric sign of blessing. In his left hand he holds a sceptre with a triple cross. Two tonsured acolytes kneel before him, one in white, one in black. The crossed keys of heaven lie at his feet on a pavement of black and white tiles. Behind him rises a heavy purple curtain embroidered with golden symbols. The bull’s head is faintly carved in the throne and also in the pillars.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The triple crown:** Dominion over the three worlds: Assiah, Yetzirah, Briah. The Hierophant bridges the spiritual and the temporal.
- **The hand gesture:** The two fingers point upward and downward, again the “as above, so below.” The hidden thumb represents the concealed middle pillar.
- **The two acolytes:** The dual aspects of the student—the purified mind (white) and the rebellious ego (black)—both seeking initiation.
- **The crossed keys:** The Keys of St. Peter, signifying the power to bind and loose, to unlock the gates of heaven and hell; the keys to the Mysteries.
- **Bull symbolism:** Taurus, the fixed earth sign, the stabilizer of spiritual doctrine; the bull as the throne of Osiris. The Hierophant is the interpreter of divine law, the anchor of tradition.
- **The purple curtain:** The veil of the temple, the garment of the priesthood, behind which lies the Holy of Holies.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Spiritual guidance, institutional religion, teaching, ritual, orthodox wisdom, the outer form of the Mysteries, mercy.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Dogmatism, hypocrisy, empty ceremony, the letter that killeth the spirit, spiritual arrogance.

### VI — The Lovers  
**Letter:** Zain (ז). **Sign:** Gemini. **Path:** 17th Path, joining Binah and Tiphereth.  
**Spiritual Title:** The Children of the Voice, the Oracle of the Mighty Gods.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A young man stands at the center, dressed in gold and green. To his right (the pillar of Mercy) stands a fair woman, her hair golden, her robe white, representing virtue. To his left (the pillar of Severity) stands a dark woman, her hair black, her robe red, representing vice or passion. Both women touch his shoulders. Above them, in a burst of golden light, the winged figure of Eros (the Archangel Raphael in some attributions) aims an arrow directly at the man’s heart. In the background, the Tree of Knowledge rises behind the dark woman, and the Tree of Life behind the fair woman, their branches intertwining. The sky is a fiery orange, the earth beneath them green.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The central man:** The conscious self (Tiphereth), poised between the influence of the Supernal Mother (Binah) and the lower self, making the great choice. His gold‑green colors are those of solar regeneration.
- **The two women:** The dual aspects of the soul—the pure aspiration (white) and the intense emotional desire (red). They represent the Gemini dilemma: the perpetual choice between the higher and lower natures.
- **Cupid/Eros:** The divine love that compels the soul to unite opposites; the arrow is the ray of inspiration from the Higher Genius. In Qabalistic terms, Zain (sword) is the weapon that divides and then unites.
- **The two trees:** The Tree of Knowledge (the dualistic, analytical world) and the Tree of Life (the unified, intuitive world), illustrating that the Lovers card is about the integration of opposites through love.
- **Path Zain:** The “sword” that cuts away false attachments, enabling the marriage of the King and Queen within.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Love, harmony, union, attraction, the need to make a crucial choice guided by the heart, the Holy Guardian Angel.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Fickleness, temptation, sentimentalism, betrayal, an inability to choose or a choice made from lower motives.

### VII — The Chariot  
**Letter:** Cheth (ח). **Sign:** Cancer. **Path:** 18th Path, joining Binah and Geburah.  
**Spiritual Title:** Child of the Powers of the Waters, Lord of the Triumph of Light.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A crowned, armored warrior stands in a chariot whose canopy is a night sky of deep blue, embroidered with silver stars. The chariot is square, formed of lapis lazuli, and its four pillars support the starry canopy. The charioteer wears a breastplate inscribed with the lunar crescent, and on his shoulders are the two Urim and Thummim. He holds a drawn sword in his right hand and a sceptre in his left. Before the chariot, two sphinxes—one black and one white—are yoked, yet no reins are visible; they are steered by the will of the charioteer alone. The chariot rests on a plain of water or a blue sphere, symbolizing the element of Cancer. In the background, a walled city is visible.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The two sphinxes:** The black and white represent the opposing forces of Severity and Mercy, contraction and expansion. They are the elemental powers of the Merkabah (chariot) of Ezekiel, tamed and directed by the will.
- **The starry canopy:** The celestial sphere, the realm of the fixed stars, showing that the charioteer’s consciousness is anchored in the heavens.
- **The armor and breastplate:** The breastplate with the lunar crescent indicates the reflective, feminine power (Cancer) channeled through the solar warrior. The Urim and Thummim are the oracular stones of the High Priest, granting perfect discernment.
- **The city:** The New Jerusalem, the sanctuary of the soul, the goal of the journey.
- **Cheth (fence/enclosure):** The Chariot is the vehicle that encloses and protects the soul while it traverses the waters of Binah (the Great Sea).
- **The water/blue sphere:** The Great Sea of the unconscious, the fluidic astral plane upon which the ego moves.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Victory, triumph over obstacles, the will directing contrary forces, conquest through balance, astral projection, rapid progress.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Overthrow, riot, rebellion, loss of control, destruction of the vehicle, selfish ambition.

### VIII — Strength  
**Letter:** Teth (ט). **Sign:** Leo. **Path:** 19th Path, joining Chesed and Geburah.  
**Spiritual Title:** Daughter of the Flaming Sword, Leader of the Lion.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A woman clad in a white robe, a garland of roses about her head and waist, bends forward and gently closes the jaws of a ferocious red lion. Her hands are not forceful; they simply rest upon the animal’s upper and lower jaws. Above her head shines the lemniscate (∞), the symbol of eternal life and spirit. The lion’s tail curls in submission. The background is a golden yellow, the color of Leo in the Queen Scale. The ground is green and fertile. The woman’s expression is calm, her gaze directed downward toward the lion, full of compassionate mastery.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The woman:** The soul, the purified feminine principle, the indwelling Shekinah. She conquers not by force but by the power of love and inner calm.
- **The lion:** Leo, the fiery kundalini force, the animal nature, the passions. The lion is red, the color of Mars, which rules the lower octave of creative fire. Its submission indicates that the animal soul is transmuted and serves the higher self.
- **The lemniscate:** The sign of the Holy Spirit, the infinite wisdom that is both above and within. It crowns the act of taming.
- **The garland of roses:** The perfection of desire. Each rose is a conquered passion, now worn as an ornament.
- **Path Teth (serpent):** The serpent of kundalini that, when tamed by Love (Chesed) balanced with Severity (Geburah), becomes the vehicle of spiritual power.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Moral courage, fortitude, inner strength, control of passions, gentle power, the capacity to overcome fear.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Pride, bullying, abuse of power, cruelty, the tyranny of the inflated ego.

### IX — The Hermit  
**Letter:** Yod (י). **Sign:** Virgo. **Path:** 20th Path, joining Chesed and Tiphereth.  
**Spiritual Title:** Prophet of the Eternal, the Magus of the Voice of Light.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
An ancient figure, cloaked in a heavy gray‑green mantle, stands on a snowy, windswept peak. In his right hand he holds a tall staff, and in his left a lantern that contains a six‑pointed golden star, shining brightly within a crystal enclosure. The lantern casts a brilliant radiance over the path before him. His face is half‑hidden by the hood, and his long white beard flows down. He appears as an archetypal sage, solitary and vigilant. The sky behind is dark, indicating twilight or the long night, making the single star in the lantern the focus of all light. A serpent sometimes coils around the staff, or the staff itself may be carved with spiral serpents.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The cloak and hood:** The concealment of the outer personality; the Hermit withdraws from the world to find the inner light. The gray‑green is the color of Virgo, the earth sign ruled by Mercury.
- **The lantern:** The Spirit within, the Holy Guardian Angel. The six‑pointed star is the hexagram of the Macrocosm, the conjunction of fire and water, spirit and matter. That he carries it in a lantern shows that wisdom must be brought down to the level of the personal self.
- **The snowy peak:** The heights of meditation, the purified intellectual plane, the cold and lonely path of the adept.
- **The staff:** The spinal column, the central channel of spiritual ascent. If a serpent coils it, it is the kundalini awakened and raised.
- **Yod:** The smallest letter, the hand of God, the seed of the Logos. The Hermit is that seed, containing all potential within a silent, concentrated form.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Prudence, introspection, deep contemplation, inner guidance, the search for truth, a spiritual teacher.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Isolation, loneliness, withdrawal from life, self‑imposed exile, pedantry, skepticism.

### X — The Wheel of Fortune  
**Letter:** Kaph (כ). **Planet:** Jupiter. **Path:** 21st Path, joining Chesed and Netzach.  
**Spiritual Title:** Lord of the Forces of Life, the Wheel of the Spirit.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A great wheel, having six or eight spokes, is mounted on a vertical post, standing upon a double‑ended boat or platform that floats on the cosmic sea. Upon the wheel, three figures are bound: a sphinx at the top, balanced and holding a sword; on the right side, a descending figure of a baboon (Cynocephalus) or Typhon; on the left side, an ascending figure of a dog‑headed man (Anubis). The wheel is adorned with the letters TARO or the alchemical symbols for salt, sulfur, mercury, and the four elements. In the four corners of the card are the four Kerubic beasts of Ezekiel: the Angel (Aquarius), the Eagle (Scorpio), the Lion (Leo), and the Bull (Taurus), each winged and holding a book. The background is of rich violet and blue, the colors of Jupiter in the King Scale.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The wheel:** The ever‑turning wheel of Samsara, the cycles of incarnation. The spokes represent the zodiacal divisions. It is the Wheel of the Law (Dharma).
- **The sphinx:** The perfected soul, the synthesis of the four elements (as the sphinx has the parts of the four Kerubic beasts), sitting unmoved at the axis of change, holding the sword of discrimination.
- **Anubis ascending:** The soul rising through the cycles, guided by the divine psychopomp. He represents the principle of evolution.
- **Typhon descending:** The soul descending into materiality, the involutionary force. Typhon is the serpent of chaos, the unregenerated energies.
- **The four Kerubic beasts:** The fixed cross of the zodiac, the foundation of the universe, the four elements in their stable aspect. They hold the books of the Akashic Records, the law written into the fabric of creation.
- **The sea:** The astral fluid, the endless possibilities of manifestation.

**Divinatory Meanings:**
- **Dignified:** Change, fortune, the turn of destiny, success, the cyclical nature of life, a propitious opportunity.
- **Ill‑dignified:** Instability, misfortune, inability to adapt, unexpected reversals, the wheel turning downward.

### XI — Justice  
**Letter:** Lamed (ל). **Sign:** Libra. **Path:** 22nd Path, joining Geburah and Tiphereth.  
**Spiritual Title:** Daughter of the Lords of Truth, Ruler of the Balance.

**Detailed Description of the Card:**
A matriarchal figure, seated upon a throne, holds in her right hand an upright sword, and in her left, a pair of perfectly balanced scales. She wears a red robe and a green mantle; upon her head is a crown with a single upright blade, and a large blue‑purple veil drapes her throne. Her expression is severe yet impartial. The throne is set between two columns (or simply placed in the center of the composition). The scales hang freely; the sword points upward without wavering. Behind her is a curtain of purple and a pavement of black and white. In some depictions, she wears a blindfold, but in the GD deck she does not—her eyes are wide open, seeing all.

**Symbolic Analysis and Deeper Appearance:**
- **The scales:** Libra, the balance of the day and night, the equilibrium of all opposing forces. The scales are empty, symbolizing the pure principle of judgment without favor.
- **The sword:** The sword of Geburah, the execution of the sentence, the active application of law. It points upward to Kether, the source of all law.
- **Red robe and green mantle:** Red (Geburah, severity) balanced by green (Venus, ruler of Libra, love and mercy). Justice is the perfect fusion of severity and compassion.
- **The crown:** The authority of the Supernal Triad, the divine right to judge. The single blade repeats the sword motif.

### XII — The Hanged Man

### XIII — Death

## Part II: The Four Suits

### The Court Cards

Each suit contains four Court Cards, representing the four sub‑elements within the parent element. They are also assigned to the Four Worlds:

- **Knight** — The fiery, active, initiating force; Atziluth.
- **Queen** — The watery, receptive, nurturing force; Briah.
- **Prince** — The airy, intellectual, communicative force; Yetzirah.
- **Princess** — The earthy, material, grounding force; Assiah.

The Court Cards thus form a complete microcosm of the Tree of Life within each suit.

---

### Suit of Wands (Fire)

**Elemental formula:**  
Knight = Fire of Fire; Queen = Water of Fire; Prince = Air of Fire; Princess = Earth of Fire.

#### Ace of Wands — Root of the Powers of Fire
*Spiritual Title:* The Root of the Powers of Fire.

**Detailed Description:**  
From a swirling bank of gray and white clouds, a right hand (or sometimes a pair of hands) issues forth, holding a massive wand that is fully aflame. The wand is broad, club‑like, with three branches and tongues of flame leaping from its entire surface. Around the wand, 22 Yods (sparks) are scattered, descending in an arc. The background is a fierce red‑orange. The wand itself is gold and red, the colors of the King and Queen Scales of Fire.

**Symbolism:**  
The hand represents the divine creative force of Kether reaching directly into Atziluth (the World of Emanation). The three branches are the Supernal Triad, the trinity of Kether, Chokmah, Binah. The leaping flames are the unbound, primordial creative Will. The 22 Yods are the paths of the Tree, indicating that all creation is latent in this single spark. This card is the pure essence of the element, before any expression into the numbered small cards.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Creative impulse, virility, will, the beginning of a great enterprise, enthusiasm.  
- Ill‑dignified: False start, blocked energy, explosive anger, the will directed toward destruction.

#### Knight of Wands — Fire of Fire
*Spiritual Title:* The Lord of the Flame and Lightning, King of the Salamanders.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A fierce warrior rides a magnificent black stallion. His armor is of gold and red, and his helmet is crested with a winged lion or a dragon. He holds a flaming wand like a spear, and his entire figure surges forward with irresistible momentum. Flames streak from the horse’s mane and tail. The landscape is a volcanic desert: cracked earth, flames venting from fissures. The sky behind him is a blaze of orange and scarlet.

**Symbols:**  
- **Black horse:** The unbridled material body, totally obedient to the fiery will; the color black absorbs all, representing the fullness of potential.
- **Flames on mane/tail:** The swift action of the element; the Knight is the embodiment of the swift creative flash.
- **Volcanic landscape:** The raw, untamed energy of the Fire World, Atziluth in its most dynamic aspect.
- **Armor:** The protection of the enlightened will; nothing can impede his charge.

**Deeper Meaning:** The Knight of Wands is the archetype of the warrior‑hero, the creative adventurer. He represents the will to act, to initiate, to conquer. His danger is recklessness and the burning out of his own energy.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Swift, enthusiastic, ambitious, daring, a leader who inspires by sheer passion.  
- Ill‑dignified: Violent, cruel, impatient, bigoted, the bully, one who exhausts all resources.

#### Queen of Wands — Water of Fire
*Spiritual Title:* Queen of the Salamanders, Lady of the Flame’s Heart.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A seated queen on a throne of golden flames, her feet on a cushion of red silk. She is robed in white and gold, with a mantle of living red. In her right hand she holds a wand from which flowers bloom; in her left hand rests a golden orb. At her feet sits a black cat, a panther, or sometimes a leopard. The background is a tropical garden or a desert oasis, with palm trees and a setting sun.

**Symbols:**  
- **Flowering wand:** The creative fire channeled into love, art, and generation.
- **Black cat:** The familiar of the Witch Queen, the lunar animal; it is the tamed shadow, the power of fascination and magnetism. As a leopard or panther, it signifies the untamable but now subservient instinct.
- **Orb:** The sovereignty of the queen over the material plane.
- **Colors:** White (purity of the inner fire), red (passion), gold (solar consciousness).

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the ruler of the heart of fire, the passionate mother, the artist. She transforms the raw flame into warmth and illumination. Her danger is a fierce possessiveness and a vindictive jealousy.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Warm, commanding, fertile in idea, generous, gracious, socially magnetic.  
- Ill‑dignified: Vindictive, domineering, proud, overly dramatic, a fiery shrew.

#### Prince of Wands — Air of Fire
*Spiritual Title:* Prince of the Chariot of Fire, Emperor of the Salamanders.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
The Prince stands in a chariot drawn by a lion or a pair of leopards. The chariot is composed of fire‑bronze, its pillars shaped like open flames. He holds a wand ending in a three‑pronged flame. He wears a winged helmet and a breastplate of scales. The landscape is an open plain under a sky of vivid violet. Streamers of flame trail from the chariot’s wheels.

**Symbols:**  
- **Chariot drawn by lions:** The fiery will (the lion) directed by the intellect (Air). He has harnessed the lions of passion to the chariot of the mind.
- **Winged helmet:** The swift, mercurial thought of the Airy part of Fire; he is the inventor, the strategist.
- **Tri‑pronged flame wand:** The three mother letters (Aleph, Mem, Shin) or the Supernal Triad, wielded as a weapon of intellect.
- **Scales on breastplate:** The element of judgment and balance; Air tempers Fire.

**Deeper Meaning:** The Prince represents the calculated, dramatic, and often theatrical expression of will. He is the showman, the evangelist, the speaker of fiery words. His danger is instability—the brilliant idea that flashes and vanishes.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Swift in decision, noble, confident, expansive, dramatic, creative intellectual.  
- Ill‑dignified: Showy, unstable, prejudiced, cruel in speech, malicious gossip.

#### Princess of Wands — Earth of Fire
*Spiritual Title:* Princess of the Shining Flame, Rose of the Palace of Fire.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A young maiden stands upon a barren, sandy earth, her feet firmly planted. She holds a tall wand that touches the sky; from its tip, a huge flame erupts. She wears a simple tunic of gold and an under‑robe of yellow. Her hair streams in the wind. Around her, small flames flicker on the ground, like spirit lights. The landscape is a plain at sunrise, the mountains in the distance touched with gold.

**Symbols:**  
- **Tall wand reaching sky:** Earth’s aspiration to become Fire, the ground of all fiery manifestation.
- **Flickering ground flames:** The fire elementals (salamanders) materializing at the Princess’s command.
- **Plain at sunrise:** The promise of the new day, the spark of fire within the material.

**Deeper Meaning:** The Princess is the final grounding of the fiery force. She is the practical enthusiast, the one who carries the spark into the world to ignite real change. Her danger is superficiality and an inability to sustain the flame.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Courageous, practical enthusiasm, the spark of genius in application, sexual vitality.  
- Ill‑dignified: Superficial, theatrical in a shallow way, unreasonable, a fire that burns itself out without achievement.

#### Knight of Cups — Fire of Water
*Spiritual Title:* Lord of the Waves and the Waters, King of the Undines.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A graceful knight rides a white horse, which moves with a gentle, gliding motion. The knight wears a winged helmet and a blue and silver armor. In his right hand he holds a cup from which a serpent rises, or sometimes a simple chalice. The horse’s trappings are scaled, like a fish. The landscape is a shoreline; the sea crashes against rocks in the background, yet the horse treads on calm, shallow water. The sky is a luminous twilight of purple and gold.

**Symbols:**  
- **White horse:** The lunar vessel, the purified emotional body, the steed of the Grail Knight.
- **Serpent in cup:** The wisdom of the deep, the kundalini in its watery aspect. It is the serpent of prophecy.
- **Winged helmet:** The spiritualized mind, the imagination soaring.
- **Shoreline:** The boundary between the conscious (land) and unconscious (sea), over which the Knight rides in perfect balance.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the romantic, the Grail Quester, driven by a fiery passion for love and beauty. He is the idealist and the artist. His danger is the intoxication of emotion; he can drown in his own visions.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Romantic, graceful, imaginative, spiritual, the lover, the artist, the bearer of good tidings of love.  
- Ill‑dignified: Sensual dreamer, unrealistic, moody, jealous, the temperamental artist.

#### Queen of Cups — Water of Water
*Spiritual Title:* Queen of the Thrones of the Waters, Lady of the Undines.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
An enthroned queen in a flowing blue robe, her throne set on the edge of a calm sea or river. She holds a large, elaborately carved cup, into which she gazes with deep absorption. The cup has two handles and is decorated with marine motifs. At her feet is a pool of still water, reflecting the moon. Her crown is of coral and shells. The sky is the deep violet of the Queen Scale of Water.

**Symbols:**  
- **The gazing into the cup:** Scrying, the vision of the true self, the reflective nature of the water element.
- **The still pool:** The tranquil subconscious, the astral mirror.
- **Coral and shell crown:** The treasures of the deep, the feminine wisdom of the sea.
- **Marine motifs:** The dolphin, the shell, the scallop—all sacred to Venus and the lunar deities.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the Great Mother of the emotional world, the ultimate empath and psychic. Her intuition is unfathomable. Her danger is a total identification with the emotional flux, becoming the victim of every passing tide.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Deep intuition, maternal love, psychic vision, emotional security, grace.  
- Ill‑dignified: Over‑emotional, unreliable, vain imagination, hysteria, the devouring mother.

#### Prince of Cups — Air of Water
*Spiritual Title:* Prince of the Chariot of the Waters, Emperor of the Undines.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
The Prince rides in a chariot drawn by a great eagle or a pair of swans. The chariot is a shell‑like vessel, floating on a river of blue and violet. He holds a cup with a winged serpent coiled around it, and in his other hand a lotus wand. He wears a tunic of blue and silver, and his helmet is crested with the wings of an eagle. The landscape is a misty, ethereal marshland at twilight.

**Symbols:**  
- **Eagle/Swans:** The eagle is the highest expression of Scorpio (Water’s fixed sign transformed), the purified emotion that becomes spiritual vision. Swans are the soul’s journey, the purity of the Grail Knight.
- **Winged serpent:** The mercurial aspect of water, the intellect that can navigate the emotions.
- **Lotus wand:** The unfolding of the spiritual heart, the awakening of the chakras through devotion.
- **Marshland:** The fertile, dream‑like realm where forms are semi‑manifest.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the subtle diplomat of the heart, the poet, the priest of the mystery cult. He uses the intellect (Air) to navigate the depths of feeling. His danger is manipulation through emotional cunning.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Cunning, subtle, artistic, spiritual intelligence, refined, meditative.  
- Ill‑dignified: Deceptive, manipulative, emotionally cold schemer, a spiritual charlatan.

#### Princess of Cups — Earth of Water
*Spiritual Title:* Princess of the Palace of the Floods, Lotus of the Palace of the Waters.

- **Willows and reeds:** The vegetation of the liminal zone between water and earth.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the Vestal Virgin of the water temple, the promise of new life and new dreams. She is the gentle, dreaming daughter of the world of Briah. Her danger is a passive, almost lazy, floating, unwilling to touch the shore of reality.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Sweet, dreamy, sensitive, gentle, fertile in imagination, the bearer of a message of love.  
- Ill‑dignified: Selfish, idle, emotionally demanding, indolent, prone to fantasy and self‑deception.

### Suit of Swords (Air)

**Elemental formula:**  
Knight = Fire of Air; Queen = Water of Air; Prince = Air of Air; Princess = Earth of Air.

#### Ace of Swords — Root of the Powers of Air
*Spiritual Title:* The Root of the Powers of Air.

**Detailed Description:**  
A hand issues from clouds, grasping a double‑edged sword. The hilt is a golden crescent, the guard two downward‑curving crescents. At the sword’s point is a golden crown, from which hang a palm branch and an olive branch. Six Vaus (nails of the divine) fall in an arc around the blade. The background is a sharp, clear blue.

**Symbolism:**  
The sword is the weapon of the intellect, the Ruach (the rational mind), issuing from Kether. The crown at the point is the sovereignty of the mind when illuminated; the palm and olive show victory and peace. The lunar crescents on the hilt indicate that the intellect must be guided by the subconscious (the Moon of Yesod). The six Vaus are the six middle Sephiroth, the world of formation.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Clarity, justice, conquest of mind, analytical power, truth cutting through illusion.  
- Ill‑dignified: Cruel speech, tyrannical thought, mental chaos, the sword that divides and wounds.

#### Knight of Swords — Fire of Air
*Spiritual Title:* Lord of the Winds and Breezes, King of the Sylphs.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A mounted warrior charges at full gallop, his horse pale gray, his armor of blue and silver. He holds a drawn sword aloft, its blade a streak of lightning. His helmet is plumed with hawk feathers. The landscape is a high, windy plateau under a sky streaked with cirrus clouds. The horse’s hooves throw up sparks from the rocky ground.

**Symbols:**  
- **Hawk feathers:** The bird of Horus, symbol of the far‑seeing intellect, the predatory mind.
- **Lightning sword:** The swift, piercing thought, the flash of genius or anger.
- **Sparks from hooves:** The ignition of air (thought) striking earth (material resistance).
- **Grey horse:** The color of the Air element in the Queen Scale.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the warrior of the mind, the logician, the righteous avenger. He acts with the speed of thought. His danger is a violent tongue and an uncontrolled temper—the fire of the air.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Protector, witty, daring, quick‑witted, skilled debater, hero of intellect.  
- Ill‑dignified: Rude, violent, rash, cruel with words, the bully‑intellectual.

#### Queen of Swords — Water of Air
*Spiritual Title:* Queen of the Thrones of Air, Lady of the Sylphs.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A regal woman, seated on a throne of clouds, holds a sword in her right hand, its point upward, and in her left hand the severed head of a man with a long beard. She wears a helmet of silver and a robe of light blue. At her feet, the clouds form the shapes of birds in flight. The sky behind her is a deep sapphire.

**Symbols:**  
- **Severed head:** The cutting off of the lower, rationalizing mind, the “old man” of the unregenerated intellect. She is the widow of the false king.
- **Upraised sword:** The mind directed toward the One, the attainment of spiritual understanding.
- **Helmet of silver:** The purity of the lunar‑mental sphere.
- **Birds in clouds:** The thoughts (sylphs) tamed and patterned.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the sorrowful wisdom gained through loss, the intellect refined by suffering. She is the clear, sharp, independent mind that has known sorrow. Her danger is bitterness, coldness, and a cutting tongue that isolates her.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Perceptive, independent, sharp mind, decisive, experienced, one who has learned through difficulty.  
- Ill‑dignified: Bitter, cold, deceptive, a widow in spirit, sorrow‑bringing, cruel.

#### Prince of Swords — Air of Air
*Spiritual Title:* Prince of the Chariot of the Winds, Emperor of the Sylphs.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
The Prince stands in a chariot drawn by two winged youths, or by a pair of eagles. The chariot is composed of swirling cloud‑forms. He holds a sword in one hand and a sickle in the other. His helmet is crested with a great wing. The landscape is a sky‑world of ever‑shifting cloud shapes and prismatic light rays.

**Symbols:**  
- **Sword and sickle:** The double action of the mind—the sword of analysis (discrimination) and the sickle of harvest (the gathering of the fruits of thought). Together they are the tools of the Magus of Air.
- **Winged youths/eagles:** The pure intellect, the angels of the Air world, the carrier pigeons of the divine message.
- **Chariot of clouds:** The mutable, formless nature of pure thought before it congeals into words.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the pure intellectual, the philosopher, the scientist. He is the ruler of the realm of ideas themselves, abstract and detached. His danger is a total disconnection from emotion and earth, a cold, calculating logic that becomes destructive.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Pure intellect, analysis, cleverness, reform, inventiveness, the ability to visualize.  
- Ill‑dignified: Cunning, scheming, unpredictable, destructive theorizing, a mercurial trickster.

#### Princess of Swords — Earth of Air
*Spiritual Title:* Princess of the Rushing Winds, Lotus of the Palace of Air.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A lithe maiden stands on a high hill, her garments whipped by the wind. She holds a sword with both hands, its point toward the earth. She wears a close‑fitting cap and a short tunic of yellow and blue. The landscape is a windy heath under a pale blue sky, with ribbons of cirrus cloud. Small birds or sylphs whirl around her.

**Symbols:**  
- **Sword held downward:** The incarnation of the intellectual principle into the material; the cutting of the roots of ignorance.
- **Wind‑whipped garments:** The restless nature of the earth of air—the urge to manifest ideas in practical form.
- **Birds/sylphs:** The elemental spirits of air, serving the Princess as the ground of the mental world.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the messenger, the eloquent speaker, the vigilant guardian of truth. She brings the airy principle down to earth, into journalism, debate, and practical analysis. Her danger is frivolity and gossip—the misuse of speech.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Dexterous, vigilant, eloquent, truth‑seeker, swift to learn.  
- Ill‑dignified: Frivolous, sly, gossipy, indiscreet, a shallow thinker.

The hand of the divine providence presenting the material world as a sacred emblem. The pentacle is the body, the microcosm, the perfected work. The white center is the stone of the wise, the incorruptible seed. The green background is the verdant earth, the field of manifestation. This is the gift of Malkuth: the kingdom in its glory.

- Ill‑dignified: Greed, materialism, wasted resources, a false sense of security.

#### Knight of Pentacles — Fire of Earth
*Spiritual Title:* Lord of the Wide and Fruitful Land, King of the Gnomes.

A heavily armored knight sits solidly on a massive, dark, shaggy horse, which stands firm rather than galloping. He holds a pentacle before him like a shield, or a scepter topped with a pentacle. His armor is of dark iron, his helmet a closed helm. The landscape is a rich, cultivated field of ripe wheat; a forest edge is visible. The sky is a warm golden‑brown.

**Symbols:**  
- **Massive horse:** The steadiness and power of the earth; the draft horse, the loyal servant of the farmer.
- **Closed helm:** The introverted, patient nature of the earth worker; he does not reveal his thoughts.
- **Cultivated field:** The fruit of labor, the transformation of wilderness into ordered plenty.
- **Iron armor:** The mineral kingdom, the bones of the earth, unbreakable endurance.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the patient builder, the farmer, the craftsman. He is the fire of earth—the slow, enduring heat of the kiln that hardens the brick. His danger is a dull, stubborn inertia that resists all change.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Steadfast, industrious, patient, reliable, a laborer who reaps the harvest.  
- Ill‑dignified: Dull, stubborn, greedy, avaricious, a plodding materialist without vision.

#### Queen of Pentacles — Water of Earth
*Spiritual Title:* Queen of the Thrones of Earth, Lady of the Gnomes.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A matriarchal queen, richly dressed in a robe of green and gold, sits enthroned in a fertile landscape. She holds a large pentacle on her lap, like a mirror. Surrounding her are sheaves of wheat, fruits, and flowers. At her feet, a hare or a dog rests. The throne is made of carved oak, and the sky is a rich amber.

**Symbols:**  
- **Pentacle as mirror:** The reflective, nourishing quality of water in earth—the awareness of the abundance of matter, the contemplation of one’s physical blessings.
- **Sheaves and fruits:** The fullness of the harvest, the generosity of the Earth Mother (Demeter/Ceres).
- **Hare:** Fertility and quick reproduction; the animal of Venus and the Moon, adding the Water of Earth principle of fecundity.
- **Oak throne:** The sacred tree of the Druids, strength and longevity.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the archetype of the nurturing provider, the practical homemaker, the woman of substance. She is the water of earth, the living water that makes the desert bloom. Her danger is a smothering materialism and a mistrust of anything intangible.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Nurturing, abundance, practical wisdom, wealth, generosity, physical health.  
- Ill‑dignified: Mistrust, neglect of higher things, avarice, a sterile materialism that hoards rather than shares.

#### Prince of Pentacles — Air of Earth
*Spiritual Title:* Prince of the Chariot of Earth, Emperor of the Gnomes.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
The Prince stands in a heavy chariot drawn by a black bull, or a pair of oxen. The chariot is built of dark wood and iron. He holds a scepter topped with a pentacle, and in the other hand an orb. His armor is of bronze, and his helmet is a bull’s head or has bull horns. The landscape is a plain of rich black soil, with distant mountains capped with snow.

**Symbols:**  
- **Black bull:** The symbol of Taurus, the fixed earth; the oxen represent plowing, cultivation, the power of slow, sustained effort.
- **Bull‑horned helmet:** The intellect (Air) applied to earth; the mind of the engineer, the architect, the logistician.
- **Bronze armor:** The alloy of copper (Venus, love of matter) and tin (Jupiter, expansion), signifying the perfected skill of the craftsman.

**Deeper Meaning:** He is the skilled artisan, the scientist of matter, the geometrician. He plans and calculates the great works of civilization: bridges, cities, monuments. His danger is a cold, cynical materialism that sees only the mechanical.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Steady worker, skilled, methodical, scientific, capable of great works of construction.  
- Ill‑dignified: Slow‑witted, overly cautious, a materialistic cynic, one who cannot see the forest for the trees.

#### Princess of Pentacles — Earth of Earth
*Spiritual Title:* Princess of the Echoing Hills, Rose of the Palace of Earth.

**Detailed Appearance:**  
A robust maiden stands in a field of tall wheat, holding a pentacle aloft like a talisman. She wears a simple crown of grain, and a tunic of brown and green. The landscape is a fertile valley surrounded by wooded hills. A great tree spreads behind her. She is often depicted as pregnant.

**Symbols:**  
- **Pentacle held aloft:** The exaltation of the material to the level of the sacred; the earth as a holy temple.
- **Crown of grain:** The mystery of Demeter and the cycle of life, death, and rebirth.
- **Pregnancy:** The Earth of Earth is the final fruition, the actual birth of the stone into the manifest world. She is the completion of the entire Tarot.
- **The great tree:** The Tree of Life itself, rooted in Malkuth.

**Deeper Meaning:** She is the final card of the pack, the materialization of all forces. She represents the embodied soul, the fully manifest human being, full of life and potential. Her danger is indolence and an animal‑like satisfaction with the purely physical.

**Divinatory Meanings:**  
- Dignified: Beautiful, fertile, gentle, trustworthy, the bringer of tangible results, full embodiment.  
- Ill‑dignified: Lethargic, self‑centered, indolent, overly attached to comfort and flesh.

### The Small Cards (Ace–10)

The thirty‑six small cards from the Two through the Ten are the **Lords of the Decanates**. Each card governs a ten‑degree segment of the zodiac, colored by the planetary ruler of that decan and the suit’s element. Their titles follow the formula “Lord of …” and describe the combined nature of the planet and sign. The Aces are the roots, the Tens are the full expression. The Golden Dawn images are rich with the sigils of the decans and the flashing colors.

---

**Decanate attributions:**  
2 — Mars in Aries; 3 — Sun in Aries; 4 — Venus in Aries; 5 — Saturn in Leo; 6 — Jupiter in Leo; 7 — Mars in Leo; 8 — Mercury in Sagittarius; 9 — Moon in Sagittarius; 10 — Saturn in Sagittarius.

**Two of Wands — Lord of Dominion**  
Mars in Aries (First Decan of Aries).  
**Appearance:** Two crossed wands, their flames interlocking; behind them a crown and a globe. The background is red‑orange.  
**Meaning:** The first crystallization of the fiery will into authority. The Martian energy in the cardinal fire sign gives absolute dominion over the material plane.  
*Dignified:* Established power, influence, mastery, a ruler or commander.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Restless ambition, arrogance, disruptiveness.

**Three of Wands — Lord of Established Strength**  
Sun in Aries (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Three wands upright, their flames steady; a ship sailing into a harbor. The sun is rising.  
**Meaning:** The solar glory of Aries; the will is now established and looks outward to commerce and exploration.  
*Dignified:* Pride, nobility, settled strength, foundation, partnerships with distant lands.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Conceit, refusal to listen, isolationist pride.

**Four of Wands — Lord of Perfected Work**  
Venus in Aries (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Four wands forming a wedding canopy or arbor, festooned with flowers and fruit. Two figures hold bouquets.  
**Meaning:** Venus softens the fiery Aries energy, bringing completion and celebration. The will’s work is done, and the feminine harmonizes the masculine.  
*Dignified:* Completion, harmony, celebration, marriage, harvest home.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Incomplete projects, a false sense of peace, premature celebration.

**Five of Wands — Lord of Strife**  
Saturn in Leo (First Decan of Leo).  
**Appearance:** Five wands, some flaming, some smoldering, brandished by five hands in chaotic conflict. No central figure.  
**Meaning:** The fiery fixed sign of Leo, restricted by Saturn, creates friction, competition, and the clashing of egos. This is the training ground of the will.  
*Dignified:* Competition, struggle, the necessary friction that precedes victory.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Turbulence, quarrels, unnecessary combat, petty strife.

**Six of Wands — Lord of Victory**  
Jupiter in Leo (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** A central wand, crowned and laureled, raised higher than the other five; the other wands are crossed in salute.  
**Meaning:** Jupiter’s expansive benevolence united with Leo’s royal fire gives public triumph and acclaim. The victorious hero returns.  
*Dignified:* Triumph, public praise, success after effort, leadership recognized.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Pride before a fall, ill‑sustained victory, the vanity of the conqueror.

**Seven of Wands — Lord of Valour**  
Mars in Leo (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Seven wands, one larger in the foreground, held by a single figure defending against the other six on a lower plane.  
**Meaning:** The martial energy of Mars, now in the fixed fire, requires courage to hold one’s ground against overwhelming odds.  
*Dignified:* Courage, defense, holding one’s ground, valour in the face of opposition.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Insecurity, defeatism, hesitation, the yielding of ground out of fear.

**Eight of Wands — Lord of Swiftness**  
Mercury in Sagittarius (First Decan of Sagittarius).  
**Appearance:** Eight wands in horizontal flight, their flames trailing behind like arrows. The landscape below is a blur.  
**Meaning:** The swiftest combination in the Tarot—Mercury, the messenger, and Sagittarius, the archer. Ideas, messages, and events fly with blinding speed.  
*Dignified:* Speed, messages, rapid action, arrows of intention, high‑velocity communication.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Missed signals, haste, chaotic quarrels, too many arrows without a target.

**Nine of Wands — Lord of Great Strength**  
Moon in Sagittarius (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Nine wands forming a palisade, a wall of living fire. The Moon casts a silver light over the scene.  
**Meaning:** The Moon (subconscious) and Sagittarius (aspiration) combine to create immense defensive resilience. The will has been tested and fortified.  
*Dignified:* Resilience, preparedness, defensive solidity, the wisdom of the subconscious protecting conscious goals.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Obstinacy, lingering damage, paranoia, a fortress that imprisons its owner.

**Ten of Wands — Lord of Oppression**  
Saturn in Sagittarius (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Ten heavy wands bound together, or a figure bent under a crushing load of wands, struggling toward a distant city.  
**Meaning:** Saturn (restriction, karma) weighing down the fiery aspiration of Sagittarius. The will has overreached and now bears the burden of its own creation.  
*Dignified:* Overburden, responsibility, hard‑won achievement, the final push before release.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Subjugation, crushing weight, refusal to delegate, the tyranny of one’s own ambition.

**Meaning:** The martial energy in Pisces finds its final expression in the perfected emotional body. Lasting joy, family, the conclusion of the emotional cycle in peace.  
*Dignified:* Lasting joy, family, emotional security, the happy ending of the grail quest.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Stagnant domesticity, falsified peace, the suppression of true feeling for the sake of outward harmony.

#### Suit of Swords (Air)

**Decanate attributions:**  
2 — Moon in Libra; 3 — Saturn in Libra; 4 — Jupiter in Libra; 5 — Venus in Aquarius; 6 — Mercury in Aquarius; 7 — Moon in Aquarius; 8 — Jupiter in Gemini; 9 — Mars in Gemini; 10 — Sun in Gemini.

**Two of Swords — Lord of Peace Restored**  
Moon in Libra (First Decan of Libra).  
**Appearance:** Two crossed swords, their points downward; a blindfolded figure sits between them, holding the hilts. The scales of Libra float above.  
**Meaning:** The lunar indecision in the cardinal air of Libra creates an enforced truce, a balancing act that holds conflict in suspension.  
*Dignified:* Balanced decision, truce, equilibrium of opposing forces, the peace of the intellect.  
*Ill‑dignified:* False peace, dishonesty, indecision, a truce masking deeper conflict.

**Three of Swords — Lord of Sorrow**  
Saturn in Libra (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Three swords piercing a heart; dark clouds and rain fill the background.  
**Meaning:** Saturn (sorrow, structure) in Libra (balance) brings the shattering of emotional balance. The truth cuts deeply; the heart is pierced by the reality of separation.  
*Dignified:* Heartbreak, separation, painful truth, the necessary surgery of the soul.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Prolonged grief, suffering in silence, alienation, the wound that festers.

**Four of Swords — Lord of Rest from Strife**  
Jupiter in Libra (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Four swords, points downward, flanking a sarcophagus upon which a knight lies in effigy. A stained‑glass window shines above.  
**Meaning:** Jupiter’s benevolence in Libra grants a respite. The battle is over; the mind must withdraw for healing and contemplation.  
*Dignified:* Recuperation, retreat, contemplation, truce, the vigil of the knight.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Isolation without healing, anxious stillness, the sleep that is not rest.

**Five of Swords — Lord of Defeat**  
Venus in Aquarius (First Decan of Aquarius).  
**Appearance:** Five swords, three held by a victorious figure who looks back in scorn; two lie broken. Two defeated figures retreat. The sky is a sickly yellow.  
**Meaning:** Venus (love) debased in the fixed air of Aquarius produces an intellectual cruelty. Victory is won by deceit or coldness, leaving a bitter taste.  
*Dignified:* Hollow victory, loss, conflict with dishonour, the testing of one’s moral code.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Vindictive despair, deep humiliation, the bully who wins and is hated.

**Six of Swords — Lord of Earned Success**  
Mercury in Aquarius (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** A ferryman poles a boat across calm water, carrying six swords in the bow. Three passengers sit in quiet dignity. The far shore is clear.  
**Meaning:** Mercury’s intelligence and Aquarian progress lead the mind from turmoil to clarity. It is the journey of science, the transition after conflict.  
*Dignified:* Transition, science, the journey after effort, a logical passage to a better state.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Stagnation in travel, dulled intellect, the journey delayed by over‑analysis.

**Seven of Swords — Lord of Unstable Effort**  
Moon in Aquarius (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** A figure sneaks away from a camp, carrying five swords; two swords are left planted in the ground. The figure wears a sly expression.  
**Meaning:** The lunar fluctuation in the fixed air of Aquarius leads to strategic but ethically questionable actions. Cunning and partial success, often involving deception.  
*Dignified:* Strategy, partial success, the need for stealth, intelligence used for self‑preservation.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Betrayal, cowardice, undisciplined plans, the theft of ideas.

**Eight of Swords — Lord of Shortened Force**  
Jupiter in Gemini (First Decan of Gemini).  
**Appearance:** A blindfolded, bound figure stands amidst eight swords planted in the ground, forming a cage. The landscape is a desolate marsh.  
**Meaning:** Jupiter’s expansion in Gemini’s mercurial mind creates a prison of over‑thinking. The force is shortened by mental bondage; the cage is of the mind’s own making.  
*Dignified:* Restriction, bondage, a crisis of thought, the acceptance of limitation.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Panic, refusal to act, self‑imposed limitation, the victim mentality.

**Nine of Swords — Lord of Despair and Cruelty**  
Mars in Gemini (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Nine swords, points downward, hang over a figure sitting up in bed, head in hands, weeping. The swords are arranged like a rack of torture.  
**Meaning:** The martial energy in the mutable air of Gemini is the cruellest combination—mental anguish, nightmares, and the torture of incessant thought.  
*Dignified:* Anguish, fear, nightmares, mental torment that precedes revelation.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Self‑pity, slander, unreasoning despair, the mind that tortures itself.

**Ten of Swords — Lord of Ruin**  
Sun in Gemini (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Ten swords, hilts protruding, pierce a prostrate body. The sky is black, but a sliver of golden dawn breaks on the horizon.  
**Meaning:** The Sun (illumination) in Gemini (the mind) brings the final, absolute collapse of the ruinous mental structure. It is the end of the cycle; only rebirth can follow.  
*Dignified:* Complete collapse, the end of a painful cycle, the intellect reduced to ashes before the phoenix rises.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Refusal to learn, lingering agony, a dramatic collapse that becomes a prolonged martyrdom.

#### Suit of Pentacles (Earth)

**Decanate attributions:**  
2 — Jupiter in Capricorn; 3 — Mars in Capricorn; 4 — Sun in Capricorn; 5 — Mercury in Taurus; 6 — Moon in Taurus; 7 — Saturn in Taurus; 8 — Sun in Virgo; 9 — Venus in Virgo; 10 — Mercury in Virgo.

**Two of Pentacles — Lord of Harmonious Change**  
Jupiter in Capricorn (First Decan).  
**Appearance:** Two pentacles connected by a figure‑of‑eight ribbon or a serpent biting its tail; a juggler balances them. Ships sail on the sea behind.  
**Meaning:** Jupiter’s expansion in the cardinal earth of Capricorn creates the ability to juggle material resources with skill and humour. Change is constant but manageable.  
*Dignified:* Adaptability, juggling resources, clever trade, the dance of material fluctuation.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Overwhelmed by fluctuation, unreliable, the buffoon who drops the plates.

**Three of Pentacles — Lord of Material Works**  
Mars in Capricorn (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Three pentacles arranged in a triangle; a craftsman works on a carved stone in a cathedral, consulting with architects.  
**Meaning:** The energy of Mars focused in Capricorn’s discipline produces the master craftsman, the builder, the one who manifests through skilled labor.  
*Dignified:* Skill, craftsmanship, diligent labour, the perfection of the trade.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Mediocrity, shoddy work, misplaced effort, the pride that ruins the work.

**Four of Pentacles — Lord of Earthly Power**  
Sun in Capricorn (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Four pentacles, arranged in the four quarters, firmly held by a figure who clings to them. A crown above, a city in the background.  
**Meaning:** The solar power in Capricorn’s material ambition gives financial security, but also the hoarding instinct. The fortress of wealth is defended.  
*Dignified:* Financial security, possession, the defensive holding of one’s gains.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Avarice, miserliness, constricted heart, the inability to enjoy wealth for fear of loss.

**Five of Pentacles — Lord of Material Trouble**  
Mercury in Taurus (First Decan of Taurus).  
**Appearance:** Five pentacles, two in the window of a lighted church, three lying in the snow. Two crippled figures walk past, ignored.  
**Meaning:** Mercury’s restlessness in the fixed earth of Taurus creates the anxiety of material lack. Spiritual sustenance is available, but the querent may not see it.  
*Dignified:* Hardship, impoverishment, spiritual thirst, the testing of faith.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Ruin, extreme worry, loss of faith, the utter despair of material failure.

**Six of Pentacles — Lord of Material Success**  
Moon in Taurus (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Six pentacles, balanced in a scale, distributed by a merchant to beggars. The background shows a prosperous town.  
**Meaning:** The lunar reflection in Taurus’s fertile earth yields a balanced exchange of resources. Generosity, charity, and the wise administration of wealth.  
*Dignified:* Generosity, charity, fair exchange, balance of resources, the patron.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Envy, miserliness under guise of charity, unfair distribution, the greedy landlord.

**Seven of Pentacles — Lord of Success Unfulfilled**  
Saturn in Taurus (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Seven pentacles on a vine, or a gardener leaning on a hoe, gazing at a shrub that bears pentacles for fruit. The crop is not yet ripe.  
**Meaning:** Saturn’s delay in Taurus’s slow growth brings the period of waiting after the labor. Patience is required; the harvest is assured if the work continues.  
*Dignified:* Patience, slow growth, reevaluation, the waiting period before fruition.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Impatience, aborted effort, wasted time, the gardener who abandons the vineyard too soon.

**Eight of Pentacles — Lord of Prudence**  
Sun in Virgo (First Decan of Virgo).  
**Appearance:** Eight pentacles, each with a different carved sigil, displayed on a workbench. An apprentice chisels a new pentacle.  
**Meaning:** The Sun illuminates the methodical sign of Virgo, bringing the perfection of skill, apprenticeship, and diligent attention to detail.  
*Dignified:* Apprenticeship, diligence, skill development, the pursuit of perfection in craft.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Drudgery, overly cautious, missed opportunity due to perfectionism, a narrow focus.

**Nine of Pentacles — Lord of Material Gain**  
Venus in Virgo (Second Decan).  
**Appearance:** Nine pentacles arranged in a garden of lilies; a lady in a rich gown stands with a falcon on her gloved hand.  
**Meaning:** Venus (beauty, love) in the analytical earth of Virgo produces refined luxury, self‑sufficiency, and the enjoyment of material culture through one’s own disciplined effort.  
*Dignified:* Refinement, self‑sufficiency, luxury through effort, the cultivated soul.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Dangerous independence, lack of discipline beneath the beauty, loss through superficiality.

**Ten of Pentacles — Lord of Wealth**  
Mercury in Virgo (Third Decan).  
**Appearance:** Ten pentacles arranged in the pattern of the Tree of Life; a patriarch and his family gather in the courtyard of a great house, flanked by hounds.  
**Meaning:** Mercury’s intelligence combined with Virgo’s perfection of the material completes the cycle. It is permanent inheritance, the establishment of the dynasty, the final material legacy.  
*Dignified:* Permanent inheritance, family abundance, tradition, the fruits of a lifetime’s work.  
*Ill‑dignified:* Family burden, stagnation through wealth, the loss of the individual in the family legacy.

## Part III: Tarot Divination

### The Significator
In a divination, a card representing the Querent is chosen as the **Significator**. Select according to the person’s sex, age, complexion, and temperament, or by their zodiacal sign:
- **Knights** for mature, active men; **Queens** for mature, receptive women.
- **Princes** for young men or those with an airy, intellectual nature; **Princesses** for young women or earthy, pragmatic persons.
- Suit is chosen by coloring and element: Wands (fair/red hair, fiery), Cups (light brown/blue eyes, watery), Swords (dark hair/eyes, airy), Pentacles (dark complexion/eyes, earthy).

### Shuffling and Cutting
1. The Querent shuffles the pack, meditating upon the question.
2. The pack is cut once with the left hand into two piles.
3. The reader reassembles the pack, placing the bottom pile upon the top.

### The Opening of the Key — The Golden Dawn Spread

**First Operation — The Unveiling of the Querent**
- Turn the entire pack face‑up. Find the Significator.
- The card to the **right** of the Significator (as you face it) is what **influences** the Querent; the card to the **left** is what **opposes**.
- If the Significator is among the first few cards, the matter is just beginning; if among the last, it nears its end.

**Second Operation — The Twelve Houses**
- After noting the Significator’s position, deal the entire pack into twelve piles, representing the astrological houses (1st House = Self, 2nd = Finances, 3rd = Communications, etc.).
- Turn each pile face‑up and read the cards as the specific influences in that area of life.

**Third Operation — The Zodiacal Circle**
- Deal the pack into a circle of twelve piles, assigning them to the signs of the Zodiac in order, starting with Aries in the ascendant position.
- Interpret the trumps, court, and small cards in each sign to give detailed personality, timing, and circumstantial indications.

**Fourth Operation — The Decanate Cards**
- From the remaining cards, or by a fresh layout, array the 36 small cards in their decanate order. This reveals the deep, elemental background forces at work, as each decan card is the “Lord” of a specific ten‑day period.

**Fifth Operation — The Tree of Life**
- Deal the pack into ten piles on the diagram of the Tree of Life (1 = Kether, 2 = Chokmah, …, 10 = Malkuth).
- Each pile reveals the forces operating through that Sephirah. Kether shows the root spiritual cause, Malkuth the final material outcome. Trumps on the Tree indicate the paths being activated; Court Cards show the persons involved; Small Cards show the circumstantial forces.

### General Significations of the Majority of a Suit
- **Many Wands:** Energy, enterprise, business, travel.
- **Many Cups:** Emotion, love, social affairs, artistic events.
- **Many Swords:** Strife, mental activity, legal matters, arguments.
- **Many Pentacles:** Money, business, material concerns, health.
- **Many Trumps:** Powerful fate, external forces beyond personal control.
- **Many Court Cards:** Many people involved; the influences of specific personalities.

**Elemental Dignities:**
A card is **well‑dignified** if surrounded by cards of its own element or a friendly element (Fire friendly with Air; Water friendly with Earth). It is **ill‑dignified** if surrounded by contrary elements (Fire vs. Water; Air vs. Earth). This greatly modifies the divinatory meanings: ill‑dignified cards are read as the reversed or shadow side of their nature.
